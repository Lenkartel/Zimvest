Where your returns come from

Four proven sources generate your earnings

ZimVest pools investor capital across diversified, actively managed instruments. Returns are distributed to investors on schedule regardless of daily market movement.

~45% of returns
Forex trading
Our licensed desk trades major currency pairs — USD/ZAR, USD/EUR, USD/GBP — using algorithmic strategies with strict daily risk limits. Zimbabwe's proximity to South Africa and regional trade corridors creates consistent short-term arbitrage opportunities that provide the largest share of our daily returns.
~45% of fund return
~30% of returns
Crypto arbitrage
Price gaps between African and international crypto exchanges — often 3–8% — are captured algorithmically in milliseconds. USDT, BTC and ETH spot arbitrage between Binance, Luno and local OTC desks provides a consistent low-risk spread. Positions are opened and closed within the same trading session, eliminating overnight exposure.
~30% of fund return
~15% of returns
Money market instruments
A portion of capital is held in USD-denominated Treasury Bills and short-duration fixed-income instruments issued by Zimbabwean corporates. These provide a stable, predictable yield layer that acts as a buffer during periods of lower trading activity, ensuring investor payouts are always met on schedule.
~15% of fund return
~10% of returns
Agricultural commodities
Zimbabwe's tobacco, gold and platinum export markets create seasonal price movements our desk trades on the Zimbabwe Mercantile Exchange. Short-term commodity futures and physical gold spot trading through Fidelity Printers and Refiners settlement round out our diversified return profile.
~10% of fund return
